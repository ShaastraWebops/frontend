About Dr.Anil Kakodkar:

Dr.Kakodkar has been a key contributor to India’s strategic nuclear programme. He was among the chosen few involved in the first successful Peaceful Nuclear Explosion Experiment that India conducted on May 18, 1974 at Pokhran.

He played a key role in the series of successful Nuclear Tests conducted during May 1998, again at Pokhran. India also demonstrated nuclear submarine powerpack technology under Kakodkar’s leadership.

 He has published over 250 scientific papers and is a distinguished Padma Trio.

He became the Director of BARC in the year 1996 and was the Chairman, Atomic Energy Commission and Secretary to the Government of India, Department of Atomic Energy, during the years 2000 -2009.

Currently he is DAE Homi Bhabha Chair Professor at BARC. Dr. Kakodkar devotes his time primarily on issues related to energy, education and societal development.

Focus of his work has been on self-reliant development of nuclear reactor systems to address the Indian programme requirements. 

He has created a roadmap for shaping the third stage of India’s nuclear power programme aimed at tapping vast energy potential of our thorium resources not only as source for electricity production but also as a primary source for other forms of energy use.

Dr.Kakodkar continues to be actively involved in programmes related to augmentation of Thorium utilisation in our nuclear power programme.

About Prof. Vivek Wadhwa:

He is founding president of the Carolinas chapter of The IndUS Entrepreneurs (TIE), a non-profit global network intended to foster entrepreneurship.

Prof. Vivek Wadhwa has done groundbreaking research about Indian Startups and their ability to succeed in such extreme conditions.

About Dr.Robert Langer :

Dr. Robert S.Langer is David H.Koch institute professor at Massachusets Institute of Technology and is the most celebrated scientist in the field of Biotechnology . He is widely recognized for research in drug delivery systems and tissue engineering.

He is the most cited engineer in the world with an astounding 151,000 citations ,holder of nearly 1000 patents and the director of the largest Biomedical Engineering lab in the world.

He is also the recipient of the U.S. National Medal of Science (2006) and U.S. National Medal of Technology and Innovation(2011) (a very rare achievement) for stellar contributions in the field of biotechnology.

Dr.Robert Langer was chief advisor on stem cell research to President Obama (then senator of the State of Illinois) in 2006.He has also held key positions on editorial boards of the top research magazines in the fields of Chemical and Biomedical Engineering.

About the Workshop :

Workshops by the TI University Programme have been loved by students all over India. So, the Shaastra 2015 Events team decided to organize a exciting 2-day hands-on workshop in IIT Madras under the TI(Texas Instruments) University Programme. 

The two day long workshop was on the topic 'Introduction to MSP430 Microcontrollers'. We had an fully hands-on workshop on C programming, popular micro-controllers and circuit designing, conducted by professionals. This free workshop was conducted by EdGate Technologies, a partner of TI University Programme, in collaboration with the CFI Electronics Club and the EE Department.
