Event Format

 

 


NOTE : Problem Statement and TDP template to be released soon !

 

This event will have on-spot registration. 
Round 1 :
It will be a written round. There will be basic technical questions as well as questions related to day to day life physics. Selected teams will be shortlisted for the second round.
Round 2 :
It will be a written test which will test the participants on design, innovation, feasibility and hands on knowledge. Selected teams will be shortlisted for the final round.
Round 3 (Finals) :
In the final round, people will be asked to build a machine (incorporating a variety of mechanical advantages) according to the problem statement, which will be released on-spot.
    Two days will be given for the teams to complete the model. Teams will be provided the access to the workshop facilities and the junkyard inside IIT Madras.Testing will be done on the final day ( or  on the third day of Shaastra  depending on the complexity of problem statement) and winning team will be decided by the working of the machine with importance given to innovation in the design and incorporation of mechanical advantages.
