Computational Neurosciences Workshop

 

At present there seems nothing more exciting than to know how the brain works. Brain is the most complex, sophisticated, and powerful information-processing device known. Computational neuroscience is a multidisciplinary endeavour pooling together methods from computer science, mathematics and physics to complement experimental research in neuroscience, and to address the analysis of complex data sets generated by the brain.

 

This workshop provides an introduction to basic computational methods for understanding what a nervous system does and for determining how it functions. You will explore the computational principles governing various aspects of vision and sensory motor control using MATLAB,  the most commonly used data analysis and visualization tool for quantitative sciences.
