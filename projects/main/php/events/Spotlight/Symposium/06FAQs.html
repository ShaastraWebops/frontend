Frequently Asked Questions

 

1. What is the IITMIS? 
The IITMIS stands for Indian Institute of Technolgy Madras International Symposium. It is a platform to discuss, debate and ideate on issues concerning the public at large. This year, we focus on the Indian Healthcare scenario and would attempt to come up with possible ways to improve it in three broad areas - the infusement of new technologies, bridging the large disparity and modifying policies. 
 

2. When and where is the IITMIS going to be held? 
The IITMIS will be held during the annual technical festival of IIT Madras, Shaastra, in the first week of January, 2015, in the IIT Madras campus. It would continue for a period of 4 days. 
 

3. What is the criterion for eligibility? 
The participants must be enrolled in an undergraduate or post-graduate course in a recognised university.
 

4. What does the IITMIS offer? 
The Symposium gives you an opportunity to work with some of the brightest minds from around the world on a problem which is of paramount concern. Mentoring sessions, ice-breaking and networking sessions, field trips and a case study competition await the participants of the symposium. 
 

5. What is the topic of The Symposium? 
Healthcare 

 

6. What aspects of Healthcare would be discussed during the Symposium? 

The Symposium would include discussions on two tracks - Health Technology and Health Policy 


6. What is the selection procedure for The Symposium?
There will be an online preliminary selection round from which the final list of participants will be released. 

 

7. What is the registration procedure like?  

  1. Visit the Shaastra login page here and register yourself for the festival
  2. Once that is done, one the dashboard, choose "Symposium" as the event and upload the answers to ONE of the problem statements (either Health Technology or Health Policy) on the dashboard. The problem statements for both tracks can be found in the resgistration tab of this website. 
  3. Click "Save Submission"

 

8. Where can the problem statements be found? 

The problem statements would be available for viewing in the 'registration' tab. 

 

9. Would I have to answer both the problem statements? 

No, you would be required to answer ONE of the two problem statements based on your interest. 

 

10. What are the problem statements about? 

The problem statement is divided into two parts - Part A and Part B.

Part A would include 4-5 short answer questions to be answered in a maximum of 100 words each. 

Part B would include three questions, out of which two must be answered in a total of 1000-1500 words. (It does not matter how the word limit is split between the two answers)

A valid entry would include BOTH parts answered and uploaded on the website in the portal provided. 


11. Could you name some partnering organizations from the years gone by? 
Some of the partners include: World Bank Urban Strategy, TERI, FICCI, IFMR, Agastya International Foundation.

 

12. Would we have to pay a REGISTRATION fee? 

Yes, all participants of the IITMIS would have to pay a nominal fee which would cover all basic expenses including accommodation, food and travel. The participants would have to FUNDtheir own travel to and from the IIT Madras campus and their home universities. 

 

13. What about accommodation and food? 

The IITMIS provides local accommodation and food to all paticipants of the symposium. Cost of travel for the field trip would be borne by the symposium as well. 

 

14. When could I know the results of the selection process? 

For international students, the results would be declared by mid-October, giving enough time for visas and tickets to be booked. 

For local students, the results would be declared in a rollng fashion, starting mid-October.
