Panel 1: Start Up Culture in India vs. Abroad

  • Kris Gopalakrishnan

​Kris Gopalakrishnan is the executive Vice Chairman and co-founder, Infosys. He is an alumni of IIT Madras. For his vision and excellent work in his field he has been awarded the Padma Bhushan in 2011, the third highest civilian honor. Not only did he startup himself, he is helping many others realize their dreams as the Chief Mentor, Startup Village. Startup Village is an incubator with an aim of supporting 1000 startups in 10 years’ time.

  • Srikanth Sundarajan

​Srikanth Sundararajan is a partner at Helion Advisors Pvt. Ltd. With 25+ years of international experience in software product and services as a part of HP, the executive leadership team at HCL and  a worldwide CTO of Cognizant. Srikanth Sundararajan has seen the whole technology-space in both US and India, and is also considered a "goldmine of information" in this aspect.

  • Anand Rajaraman

​Anand Rajaraman is an alumnus of IIT Madras and later completed his MS and PhD in Computer Science from Stanford. He is the founder of former Junglee Corp. and later was the Director of Technology at Amazon.com. His role was decisive in transforming Amazon from a retailer to retail platform. In 2000, he founded Cambrian Ventures, which backed companies later acquired by Google.

 

 

Panel 2: Starting Up vs. Climbing the Corporate Ladder

  • Uma Ghurka

Uma Ghurka was the former President of the Confederation of Women Entrepreneurs and was recognized as one of the top 50 women entrepreneurs in 1999. She started up at a time when starting up was not even considered to be an option. She boldly took the road less travelled, deciding to explore opportunities to develop and manufacture new products and eventually set up her own manufacturing facility, Thermopads Pvt. Ltd., in Hyderabad in 1978.

  • Rajiv C Lochan

​Rajiv C Lochan is currently the MD and CEO, The Hindu. He has also had a previous stints as an ex-partner at McKinsey & Company and American Express. His background gives a very exciting angle to the discussion which is sure to enthrall the diverse audience.​​

  • Dr. Sunil Handa

Having graduated as an electrical engineer and a topper of his batch at IIMA, Dr. Sunil Handa started a pharmaceutical company, Core Emballage. He conducts workshops on Entrepreneurial Motivation, which has motivated and inspired many budding entrepreneurs.

  • Laks Krishnamoorthy

Laks Krishnamoorthy is the Director, Reporting Centre of Excellence at PayPal India. He has 24+ years’ experience in building, leading teams that have developed medium to complex real time solutions with cross functional global teams. He has worked for Tata Unisys and Sabre before. He specializes in building large-scale systems that operate at real time.
