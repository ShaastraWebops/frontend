Event Format

Round 1 - Submission of TDP

  • Every team is required to produce a write-up describing the basic idea employed in designing and building the glider.
  • In Team Description paper (TDP) the teams have to answer a subjective questionnaire related to your design of the glider.
  • It will have general questions related to roll, pitch, yaw etc. and how the participants plan to maximise the payload carried by their aircraft.
  • Some approximate aircraft parameters must be mentioned.
  • Note - Originality in the design will be the basis for selection of teams for the event. Submission must be in the format we mention.

 

Round 2 - Presentation Round

  • Shortlisted teams from the first round will be invited to IIT Madras during Shaastra 2015 (3rd-6th January).
  • Every team must give a power point presentation (ppt) before a panel of judges.
  • The presentation should incorporate the idea, aircraft parameters and uniqueness of the design.
  • The presentation must also explain how exactly you arrived at the design, and the design parameters chosen. In a nutshell your presentations must be informative and prove your worthiness.
  • The final points for this part will be given only after a keen assessment of the design by the judges.  
  • A PowerPoint presentation template will be available on the website soon.

 

Round 3 - Flight Testing Round

  • The glider shall be launched by using variable angle slingshot mechanism launcher (see Problem Statement) provided by coordinators.
  • The glider should consist of features mentioned during the presentation round. Failing to do so will have the teams penalised.
  • Teams should note that the place where flight testing is done is an open area; hence changes in wind direction may occur. Teams should keep this in mind while designing the glider.
